project summary

Exploration of Effective Visualization Techniques for Storylines

vision

If we are to effectively discover similar causal patterns in events, we must first be able to discover and describe these patterns in a given set of series of events and be able to communicate their parallels effectively. Constructing novel interactive visualizations linking series of causal events in stories enables selective depiction of various story elements. This provides a way to explore what elements contribute to similar story structure over a set of stories, and offers an avenue for discovery of common elements over two stories that lead to similar structure via the human visual system.

motivation

  • Stories are very data dense, which makes it hard to pick up patterns.
  • Timelines aren't always adequate ways of displaying multi-dimensional data.
  • We might want to be able to artificially learn from experience.
  • We might want to make the most of our own pattern detection abilities.

Project Type: Research with Implementation

steps

  • Classify data types and explore effective methods of visualization as applied to these data types, and how they may be combined or extended effectively into a basic set of visualization templates
  • Construct templates and input data
  • Identify potential combinations of data that depict consistent story structures among stories that have been previously identified as similar in order to benchmark whether visualizations display similar patterns over different, but "similar" stories.

contributions

  • Introduces novel method of story visualization
  • Tests efficacy in showing similar structures over "similar" stories
  • Identifies a potential method of identifying and communicating similar story structure over arbitrary sets of events in Genesis System
